Key Considerations When Buying an MRI Scanner

Choosing an MRI scanner requires careful assessment. Consider the following points:

  • Magnetic Field Strength: Decide if you need a high-field 3T MRI system or if a 1.5T MRI would suffice, depending on the level of detail required.
  • Open vs. Closed Systems: Choose open MRI systems for patients with claustrophobia, or for children.
  • Gradient System: Check the gradient performance of the scanner, which will give more detail in the images.
  • Patient comfort: Check that the system has a comfortable table for the patients.
  • Installation Requirements: Check the space and power requirements before purchasing.
  • Maintenance and Support: Verify that the company offers proper maintenance for your region.

How to Select an MRI Manufacturer

Choosing a reliable manufacturer is essential for long-term performance and dependability. Make sure to consider the following points:

  • Experience: Check the experience of the manufacturer in producing MRI equipment.
  • Certifications and Approvals: Check if the company has all the required certifications and licenses.
  • Customer Feedback: Search for customer reviews and testimonials.
  • Innovation: Choose a manufacturer that is known for innovation.
