Key Considerations When Buying a CT Scanner

Choosing the right CT scanner for your needs requires careful consideration. Here are some key points to keep in mind:

  • Number of Slices: Determine if you need a high-slice scanner for advanced imaging (like 64 or 128 slices) or if a basic 4 or 16 slice scanner will suffice.
  • Radiation Dose: Look for scanners that minimize radiation exposure while providing high-quality images.
  • Applications: Make sure that the scanner is suitable for your intended use case (e.g., cardiology, oncology, general diagnostic imaging).
  • Image quality: Review some sample images to ensure the image clarity meet your requirements.
  • Installation Requirements: Check the space, power, and infrastructure needs for the installation.
  • Service & Maintenance: Ask about maintenance and support options available for the region.

How to Select a CT Manufacturer

Purchasing from a reputable manufacturer is crucial to guarantee the long-term performance and reliability of your equipment. When evaluating manufacturers, make sure to consider:

  • Years of experience: Prioritize manufacturers that have been producing CT equipment for a good amount of time.
  • Certifications and approvals: Check that the manufacturer has the needed certifications to distribute in your country.
  • Customer reviews and testimonials: Research real user experiences, and check if the company has any bad reviews.
  • Product range: Verify if the manufacturer has the type and models that you need.
